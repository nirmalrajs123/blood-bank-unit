Frequently Asked Questions on Blood Donation

1.What are some quick facts about blood donation?

Red blood cells have a short shelf life. They only last for 6 weeks (42 days). Donating whole blood takes only about 10-15 minutes. You can donate whole blood every 56 days—and we encourage you to donate as often as possible.

2.Who Cannot donate blood?

You must be in good health at the time you donate. You cannot donate if you have a cold, flu, sore throat, cold sore, stomach bug or any other infection. If you have recently had a tattoo or body piercing you cannot donate for 6 months from the date of the procedure.

3.What is the benefits of donating blood?

A healthier heart and vascular system
Regular blood donation is linked to lower blood pressure and a lower risk for heart attacks. “It definitely helps to reduce cardiovascular risk factors,” says DeSimone
